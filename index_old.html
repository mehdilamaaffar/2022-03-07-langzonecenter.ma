À PROPOS

Language zone Ouvert en 2009 Agrée par le Ministère de l’Education Nationale :

ENSEIGNEMENT DES LANGUES

La maîtrise des langues étrangères et l’esprit d’ouverture à l’international font partie des éléments majeurs d’une insertion professionnelle réussie. Se former rapidement à une langue étrangère est de ce fait devenu une nécessité incontournable.

TRADUCTION

Nos traducteurs sont des professionnels expérimentés et diplômés dans la traduction. Ils traduisent généralement d'une langue source vers leur propre langue.

SÉJOURS LINGUISTIQUES

L’immersion linguistique permet de découvrir une culture tout en apprenant la langue dans un environnement favorable à la pratique orale ou écrite.

COURS DE SOUTIEN

Langzone propose des cours particuliers dans toutes les matières afin d'aider les élèves à optimiser leurs résultats à l'école. Du primaire aux études supérieures en passant par le collège et le lycée.

Services

Host Families

Students will be placed in host families and given the opportunity to learn about Moroccan culture in one of the fastest and most meaningful ways. Students are encouraged to use as much Darija as possible during the homestay. Their families––at least in the beginning––will be their most significant connection to Moroccan culture.

Classical Arabic (Fush`a) Classes

Although it is not spoken in everyday life, Modern Standard Arabic (MSA), otherwise known as Fus’ha, is the common Arabic across the Arab world and is used for formal reading and writing like news broadcasts and formal situations. This exchange program will offer a variety of Fus’ha classes based on the level of the student. *Students who are at an advanced level will be given the option of private tutoring.

Moroccan dialect(Darija) classes

The Moroccan Arabic dialect, colloquially known as Darija, is a mixture of Fus’ha, Berber (Amazigh) dialects, French, and Spanish. It is generally thought to be one of the dialects farthest from Fus’ha in that much of its words and grammar derive from other languages, particularly the Berber ones.

Orientation sessions

Host Families orientation

The program will have a host family orientation in order to prepare the students for cultural expectation in the family.

Street Harassment orientation

Unfortunately, street harassment is an ongoing issue in Morocco––as it is in the majority of the world. In order to prepare students for this reality, the program will hold an orientation on street harassment that pertains to both males and females. Students will hear from young Moroccans about the issue

Bargaining orientation

In Morocco, bargaining––especially in markets––is expected. Only in retail stores and restaurants is bargaining not used. Westerners tend to be overcharged, due to the stereotype that they are wealthy. In this orientation, students will learn about expectations in bargaining and will learn different tactics for how to effectively bargain.

Lectures

Every week, there will be lectures on a number of different topics regarding Morocco. There will be a general overview about Morocco in addition to lectures relating to women’s empowerment, religion (particularly Islam and Judaism), and Moroccan politics

Teaching methods

The teaching method at LangZone is based on a communicative approach that aims at enhancing the communicative skills of the participants as a major concern. Four language-learning skills are adopted: reading, writing, listening and speaking.

LangZone club

Participants meet twice a week with Arabic speaking students to discuss themes related to the Moroccan culture in the targeted language to improve participants’ communication skills as well as familiarize them with Moroccan culture.
