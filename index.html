Teaching methods

The teaching method at LangZone is based on a communicative approach that aims at enhancing the communicative skills of the participants as a major concern. Four language-learning skills are adopted: reading, writing, listening and speaking.

LangZone club

Participants meet twice a week with Arabic speaking students to discuss themes related to the Moroccan culture in the targeted language to improve participants’ communication skills as well as familiarize them with Moroccan culture.

Moroccan dialect (Darija) classes

The Moroccan Arabic dialect, colloquially known as Darija, is a mixture of Fus’ha, Berber (Amazigh) dialects, French, and Spanish. It is generally thought to be one of the dialects farthest from Fus’ha in that much of its words and grammar derive from other languages, particularly the Berber ones.
